# Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants, algae, and some
bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose. It
takes place in the chloroplast, where the pigment chlorophyll absorbs
sunlight. The overall reaction combines carbon dioxide and water, driven by
light, to produce glucose and release oxygen as a byproduct.

The process has two stages. The light-dependent reactions capture photons
and produce ATP and NADPH. The light-independent reactions, also called the
Calvin cycle, use that ATP and NADPH to fix carbon dioxide into sugar.
