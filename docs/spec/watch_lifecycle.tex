\section{Carrier and abstraction}

A single database is modelled; the daemon's route key $(database, collection)$
collapses to a collection, because $directories.collection$ is unique within a
database and every invariant of interest is stated per route key.  The
directory tree and its ancestor relation --- the layer that
\textit{SyncRegistry} enforces --- are abstracted away: their only effect on the
daemon state is the set of collections a register call \textit{subsumes}, which
enters the model as the input $subsumed?$.  Three collections suffice to exhibit
a parent, a distinct child, and a same-name self-subsume.

\begin{zed}
  COLL ::= ca | cb | cc
\end{zed}

\section{State}

The lifecycle state records, per collection, five facts: whether it is
registered (has a $directories$ row), whether the loop watches it, whether a
shed orphan-purge is deferred against it, whether its chunks are present in
LanceDB, and whether its chunks were deliberately retained by a
$keep\_data$ deregister.  The schema carries typing only: no safety predicate
is baked in, so every operation is feasible into any type-correct after-state
and a safety violation, if one is reachable, is actually reached rather than
silently pruned by the schema of the primed state.

\begin{schema}{Watch}
  registered : \power COLL \\
  watched : \power COLL \\
  pending : \power COLL \\
  chunks : \power COLL \\
  retained : \power COLL
\end{schema}

Initially nothing is registered, watched, pending, chunked, or retained.

\begin{schema}{Init}
  Watch \\
  \where
  registered = \emptyset \\
  watched = \emptyset \\
  pending = \emptyset \\
  chunks = \emptyset \\
  retained = \emptyset
\end{schema}

\section{The safety invariant}

The five properties the design must maintain are collected in $SafetyOK$.  It is
\textit{not} an ancestor of any operation --- it is the predicate whose negation
the model checker searches for as a reachable state.

\begin{schema}{SafetyOK}
  Watch \\
  \where
  registered \cap pending = \emptyset \\
  chunks \setminus (registered \cup retained) \subseteq pending \\
  registered \subseteq watched \\
  watched \cap pending = \emptyset
\end{schema}

The four conjuncts are, in order, I1, I2, I3 and I5.  I1: a registered
collection never carries a deferred purge.  I2: a collection whose chunks exist
while it is neither registered nor deliberately retained is tracked for purging.
I3: a registered collection is watched (a null observer handle is still a
watched key; the reconcile disk-scan is its correctness backstop).  I5: a
watched collection --- one whose observer feeds inserts --- never simultaneously
carries a deferred purge, so the single writer per route key is never split
between an inserting watch and a deleting purge.  I4 (reconcile never tears down
a live watch) is a property of the $Reconcile$ transitions rather than of a
single state, and is discharged below by observing that each preserves I3.
I6 (the disk-derived sweep deletes only true orphans --- never a registered or
retained collection) is likewise a property of the $Sweep$ transition, discharged
by its delete set $chunks \setminus (registered \cup retained)$, which excludes
$registered \cup retained$ by construction.

\section{Producing chunks}

A watch or scan turns files on disk into chunks.  Chunks may appear for any
registered collection.

\begin{schema}{IndexProduce}
  \Delta Watch \\
  c? : COLL \\
  \where
  c? \in registered \\
  chunks' = chunks \cup \{ c? \} \\
  registered' = registered \\
  watched' = watched \\
  pending' = pending \\
  retained' = retained
\end{schema}

\section{Register}

Registration resolves the client directory, writes the $directories$ row, and
--- because $directories.collection$ is unique and register subsumes every
existing registration whose directory is a descendant of the new one --- may
delete a set $subsumed?$ of rows in the same transaction.  The parent's own name
appears in $subsumed?$ exactly in the same-name self-subsume case ($c? \in
subsumed?$).  Each subsumed child is torn down and its chunks purged
\textit{before} the parent watch is installed; a purge the saturated queue sheds
($shed?$) is deferred for a reconcile retry.  Installing the parent watch runs
$begin\_collection$, which discards any deferred purge standing against the
parent --- so a live re-registration supersedes a stale deferred purge.

\begin{schema}{Register}
  \Delta Watch \\
  c? : COLL \\
  subsumed? : \power COLL \\
  shed? : \power COLL \\
  \where
  subsumed? \subseteq registered \cup \{ c? \} \\
  shed? \subseteq subsumed? \\
  registered' = (registered \setminus subsumed?) \cup \{ c? \} \\
  watched' = (watched \setminus subsumed?) \cup \{ c? \} \\
  chunks' = chunks \setminus (subsumed? \setminus shed?) \\
  pending' = (pending \cup shed?) \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  retained' = retained \setminus (\{ c? \} \cup subsumed?)
\end{schema}

The purge of a non-shed child clears its chunks; a shed child keeps its chunks
but is placed in $pending$, so it stays tracked.  The parent is removed from
$pending$ last, capturing the discard that lets a self-subsume whose purge shed
still leave the now-live parent free of a deferred purge.

\section{Deregister}

Deregistration removes the row synchronously, stops the watch \textit{before}
any purge so no in-flight event re-enqueues the collection mid-purge, and then
--- unless $keep\_data$ was asked --- purges the chunks through the per-key
FIFO.  The purge is admitted with a bounded retry; its two outcomes are distinct
operations.

The purge is admitted and drains the chunks.

\begin{schema}{DeregisterPurge}
  \Delta Watch \\
  c? : COLL \\
  \where
  c? \in registered \\
  registered' = registered \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  watched' = watched \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  chunks' = chunks \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  pending' = pending \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  retained' = retained \setminus \{ c? \}
\end{schema}

The queue stays saturated past the admission deadline, so the purge is never
admitted.  Symmetric with the subsume path, the deregister path defers the
failed purge to $pending$ for a reconcile retry, so the still-chunked collection
stays tracked until a later sweep admits the delete.

\begin{schema}{DeregisterPurgeShed}
  \Delta Watch \\
  c? : COLL \\
  \where
  c? \in registered \\
  registered' = registered \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  watched' = watched \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  chunks' = chunks \\
  pending' = pending \cup \{ c? \} \\
  retained' = retained \setminus \{ c? \}
\end{schema}

With $keep\_data$ the chunks are kept on purpose; the collection joins
$retained$ and no purge is scheduled.

\begin{schema}{DeregisterKeep}
  \Delta Watch \\
  c? : COLL \\
  \where
  c? \in registered \\
  registered' = registered \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  watched' = watched \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  chunks' = chunks \\
  retained' = retained \cup \{ c? \} \\
  pending' = pending \setminus \{ c? \}
\end{schema}

\section{Begin collection}

$begin\_collection$ installs a watch for a registered collection and discards any
deferred purge standing against it.

\begin{schema}{BeginCollection}
  \Delta Watch \\
  c? : COLL \\
  \where
  c? \in registered \\
  watched' = watched \cup \{ c? \} \\
  pending' = pending \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  registered' = registered \\
  chunks' = chunks \\
  retained' = retained
\end{schema}

\section{Purge retry}

The reconcile drain re-submits each deferred purge whose collection is no longer
registered; a collection that is registered again is dropped, never purged, lest
the drain wipe the live collection's fresh chunks.  The two branches are
distinct operations.

The target is still absent: the purge is re-submitted and drains the chunks.

\begin{schema}{PurgeRetryAbsent}
  \Delta Watch \\
  c? : COLL \\
  \where
  c? \in pending \\
  c? \notin registered \\
  chunks' = chunks \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  pending' = pending \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  registered' = registered \\
  watched' = watched \\
  retained' = retained
\end{schema}

The target was re-registered: it is dropped from $pending$ without a purge.

\begin{schema}{PurgeRetryLive}
  \Delta Watch \\
  c? : COLL \\
  \where
  c? \in pending \\
  c? \in registered \\
  pending' = pending \setminus \{ c? \} \\
  chunks' = chunks \\
  registered' = registered \\
  watched' = watched \\
  retained' = retained
\end{schema}

\section{Disk-derived sweep}

The durable backstop derives orphans from actual state on every reconcile: it
deletes the chunks of every collection that is neither registered nor retained.
Unlike the pending drain it needs no in-process record, so it survives a restart
and cleans up any shed purge --- deregister or subsume --- whose chunks were left
behind.  Its delete set is exactly $chunks \setminus (registered \cup retained)$;
a registered or deliberately-retained collection is therefore never swept, which
is I6.  The blocking chunk scan and the retained read run off the event loop; the
delete decision uses the live roster snapshot for $registered$ and the durable
$keep\_data$ marker for $retained$.

\begin{schema}{Sweep}
  \Delta Watch \\
  \where
  chunks' = chunks \cap (registered \cup retained) \\
  registered' = registered \\
  watched' = watched \\
  pending' = pending \\
  retained' = retained
\end{schema}

The swept set $chunks \setminus chunks'$ equals $chunks \setminus (registered
\cup retained)$, so the sweep preserves I2 (it purges exactly the orphans I2
requires to be tracked) and cannot delete a registered or retained collection's
chunks.

\section{Reconcile}

A reconcile pass adds a watch for each registered collection that lacks one,
rescans the rest, and --- only on a fully enumerated roster --- tears down every
watch whose registration has disappeared and drains the deferred purges,
skipping any collection that is live again.

On a complete enumeration the current roster is exactly $registered$.  Every
registered collection ends watched (add-missing plus survive-teardown); every
watch of a gone collection is removed; every deferred purge of an absent
collection is drained (chunks cleared) and every deferred purge of a live
collection is dropped --- so $pending$ empties.

\begin{schema}{ReconcileComplete}
  \Delta Watch \\
  \where
  watched' = registered \\
  pending' = \emptyset \\
  chunks' = chunks \setminus (pending \setminus registered) \\
  registered' = registered \\
  retained' = retained
\end{schema}

If the drain's re-submission sheds, the absent deferred purges stay pending and
their chunks remain; the live ones are still dropped.

\begin{schema}{ReconcileCompleteDrainShed}
  \Delta Watch \\
  \where
  watched' = registered \\
  pending' = pending \setminus registered \\
  chunks' = chunks \\
  registered' = registered \\
  retained' = retained
\end{schema}

The enumeration raised partway: only a prefix $seen?$ of the roster was visited.
The add / rescan already ran for what enumerated --- so those collections gain a
watch and shed their deferred purge --- but every removal is skipped, because a
partial roster would make a live collection look absent.

\begin{schema}{ReconcilePartial}
  \Delta Watch \\
  seen? : \power COLL \\
  \where
  seen? \subseteq registered \\
  watched' = watched \cup seen? \\
  pending' = pending \setminus (seen? \setminus watched) \\
  registered' = registered \\
  chunks' = chunks \\
  retained' = retained
\end{schema}
